Digital surfaces do not need to imitate paper. They need to remember that reading is physical even when the material is light.

Texture, delay, and restraint become a way of restoring weight without nostalgia.

> [!tip]+ Borrow pressure, not ornament
> Interfaces feel tactile when they acknowledge pacing, friction, and consequence.
