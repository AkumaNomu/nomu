I stole the keys, I can finally relax. It's good to disconnect sometimes, I have a temporary safe spot for now, it's not gonna last long but I'm planning on finding another safe spot potentially.

Freedom is good, not just freedom of actions, but freedom of thought too. Having all the noise gone is very good; the sound isn't totally gone, it's just different. Difference was always good.

I'm gonna try to keep it up for a week to see how it goes, but i'll have to find a way to sustain it and pay my dues, I'll see about that. I have some savings on the side to keep me afloat for a week or two, but I'll see. I have to get things much quicker.

I've been thinking about mental resets since Sunday, when I met him. He needed it. Some sun, greenery, waves, and samsa and the strokes did a good job, but it got me thinking. I never really show it, but I need a reset a lot of times. I try to do it, but it's limited. This is my solution to the reset.

Typing is really cool, I missed laptop keyboards. I miss my old laptop in reality, I have never been the type to miss inanimate objects - or even animate ones, but I'll digress - but I felt something strange when I saw it again, and when I got the bad news.

This one is fine, it gets a job done. Gets a little too hot sometimes but it feels nice changing. I like change, atleast in certain things. But someone has pointed out to me that I like repetition. It's true. I think I like repetition to really feel the change, otherwise it's always a constant road, not a loop to break out of. Some people might hate being in a loop but I love it, as long as it's my loop, or at least partially own. He owns the boulder, and I own the loop. He rolls it up from a different side each time, and I walk through it in different directions.
