The grid proposes that communication should be accountable to proportion. It replaces visual impulse with a system of promises.

This does not make the grid neutral. It gives it a moral tone: an insistence that information should be disciplined before it is decorative.

| Principle | Promise |
| --- | --- |
| Alignment | The page can be trusted |
| Rhythm | Reading can become predictive |
| Repetition | Structure can outlast mood |

There is an implicit theorem under the grid:

$$
clarity \neq truth
$$

The system can illuminate content, but it can also overstate the confidence of that content. [[architecture-of-silence]] accepts a looser argument: that form can guide without pretending to settle everything.
