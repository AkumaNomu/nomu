Friction is not always a defect. Sometimes it is the surface that lets thought grip the page.

A completely seamless experience risks turning attention into throughput. The archive refuses this by asking the reader to slow down.

- Friction can be a cue.
- Delay can be an editorial decision.
- Completion is not the only measure of value.
