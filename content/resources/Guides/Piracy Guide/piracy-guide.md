# What even is torrenting?
Torrenting is a file distribution protocol called *BitTorrent*.
It’s a way to move large files across the internet without destroying a single server.

- Instead of:
**One server → Everyone downloads**
- It works like:
**Everyone → Everyone** (Peer 2 Peer)

You download pieces from strangers.
Strangers download pieces from you. It's as simple as that.

## Protection (Optional)
When you torrent, your IP address is visible to everyone. A lot of ISP's ( Internet Service Providers ) in western countries can get you in trouble for illegal piracy, but that depends on your country of residence. 3rd world countries are generally safe, but 

### How to use a VPN
Get a VPN that:
- Allows P2P traffic 
- Has a kill switch (if your vpn stops working, it wont leak your IP)
- Doesn't keep logs of your activity

*Popular choices are often Mullvad, ProtonVPN, AirVPN*

### Bind qBittorrent to your VPN 
This ensures torrents ONLY use your VPN connection, but it usually depends on how your VPN is setup, generally:
1. Go to Tools → Preferences → Advanced
2. Find "Network interface"
3. Select your VPN adapter (not your regular WiFi/Ethernet)
4. If the VPN disconnects, qBittorrent stops immediately

### What to look for in a torrent:
- **Seeders**: People uploading. More = faster downloads
- **Leechers**: People downloading. Some is fine, too many might mean slow speeds
- **Comments/Reviews**: Check if anyone's screaming "VIRUS"
- **Uploader reputation**: Trusted uploaders have history

## Step 4: Verify before you open
Malware loves to dress up as movies and software.

### For executable files (.exe, .msi, .app):
- Check file size – A 50MB "Photoshop" is definitely malware
- Look for portable versions when possible
- Scan with VirusTotal before running

### General safety rules:
- Avoid ".exe" files in movie/TV show torrents
- Read comments before downloading
- If it seems too good to be true (new game for free), it probably is

## Step 5: Seed or sink
Once you've downloaded, leave the torrent running.
This is "seeding" – giving back to the community.

**Why seed?**
- Keeps the swarm alive
- Good karma
- Required on private trackers
- Makes downloads faster for everyone

Even seeding to 1.0 ratio (uploaded as much as you downloaded) helps enormously.
